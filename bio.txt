Andrés Monroy-Hernández is the director of the Human-Computer Interaction Lab at Princeton, a faculty member in the Princeton Department of Computer Science, and a Principal Research Scientist at Snap. He builds and studies technologies that help millions of people connect and collaborate in new ways, including MIT's Scratch online community, Microsoft's first hybrid AI agent, and Snapchat's co-located AR experiences. Andrés was named one of the 35 Innovators under 35 by the MIT Technology Review magazine in Latin America and one of the most influential Latinos in Tech by CNET. Andrés holds a master's and Ph.D. from MIT, and a BS from Tec de Monterrey in México. He also serves on the board of Crisis Text Line.
